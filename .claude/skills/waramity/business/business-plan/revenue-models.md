# Revenue Model Patterns

Use this reference to pick and configure the right revenue model(s) for each business plan.

---

## Pattern 1: Freemium Mobile App

**Best for:** Consumer apps with large addressable market, low switching cost.

Benchmark conversion rates (industry average):
- Free → Paid: 2–5% (casual apps), 5–10% (productivity/tools)
- Monthly churn on paid: 3–8%
- Annual plan discount that maximizes LTV: 30–40% off monthly

Pricing guidance (Thailand market, 2025):
- Low price point: $29–49/mo (impulse buy, no hesitation)
- Mid price point: $99–149/mo (clear value needed)
- High price point: $299+/mo (B2B mindset, needs ROI story)

---

## Pattern 2: B2B SaaS

**Best for:** Tools that save businesses time or reduce headcount cost.

Benchmark conversion rates:
- Free trial → Paid: 15–25%
- Monthly churn: 1–3% (much lower than B2C)
- Sales cycle: 1–4 weeks for SME, 1–6 months for enterprise

Pricing guidance (Thailand SME market):
- Per-seat: $299–990/user/mo
- Per-company flat: $990–2,990/mo (up to 10 users)
- Enterprise: $5,000+/mo (custom)

LTV:CAC ratio target: >3:1

---

## Pattern 3: Affiliate / Referral

**Best for:** Financial, insurance, investment apps where user action = partner revenue.

Typical commission rates:
- Insurance lead: $200–500 per completed application
- Investment fund: 0.5–2% of initial investment
- Credit card: $500–2,000 per approved card
- Loan referral: 0.5–1% of loan amount

Requirements:
- Must disclose affiliate relationship clearly (SEC/FCA rules)
- Only recommend after user has completed their core task (non-intrusive)
- Works best when recommendation is personalized to user's data

---

## Pattern 4: Marketplace / Transaction Fee

**Best for:** Two-sided platforms connecting buyers and sellers.

Typical take rates:
- Services marketplace: 10–20%
- Physical goods: 5–10%
- Digital goods: 15–30%

Critical mass problem: Need both supply and demand. Always launch supply-side first.

---

## Pattern 5: One-Time Purchase (Paid App)

**Best for:** Utility apps with no ongoing server costs, tools with clear finite value.

Considerations:
- No recurring revenue — requires constant new user acquisition
- Works if: offline, no backend, clear utility, word-of-mouth product
- Thailand price range: $99–349 one-time
- Pairs well with: free lite version + paid full version

---

## Pattern 6: Data / API Licensing (Year 2+)

**Best for:** Apps that accumulate valuable aggregated/anonymized data.

Revenue potential: $50K–500K/yr per enterprise API customer.
Requirements: Meaningful data volume, strong privacy compliance, B2B sales motion.
Do not plan for this in Year 1. List as "Year 2 opportunity."

---

## Combining Models

Most successful apps use 2–3 models:

| Primary | Secondary | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Freemium | Affiliate | Finance app: free tier + recommend RMF funds |
| Freemium | B2B | Consumer app that sells HR version to companies |
| One-time | IAP | Paid base app + unlock extra features |
| Free | Subscription | Free individual + team subscription |

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **Pricing too low in Year 1** — Harder to raise prices later. Start at a price you're slightly uncomfortable with.
2. **Over-relying on ads** — Ad revenue for small apps is negligible (<$5K/mo at 100K users). Don't lead with this.
3. **No annual option** — Annual plans reduce churn 40–60%. Always offer both.
4. **Gating wrong features** — Gate features that deliver ongoing value (reports, history, sync), not one-time features.
5. **Free tier too generous** — If free users never hit a wall, they never convert. Design a clear "aha moment" that requires paid.
